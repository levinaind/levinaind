hang on!

a mobile app that allows user to swap and upcycle clothes in effort to stop fast fashion

Project Type

Designathon

Role

UX Design

Teammates

Pery Au
Therina Castor

Duration

3 weeks (Mar 2021)

Tools

Figma

Overview

How might we create social transformation that shifts users towards greener behaviour and sustainable practices?

During the 3-week Laurier’s UX Design for Change Challenge, my team created hang on, a technological solution to stop fast fashion.

Problem

Fast fashion has became a huge problem over the past few decades as it is responsible of the earth’s global carbon emissions. People tend to shop for trend instead of shopping sustainably, which causes a constant influx in textile production and unwanted clothing items, which in the end ends up in the landfill. We know that if our society keeps depending on the fast fashion industry, we will be left with no Earth one day.

Solution

With hang on, my team presented different methods to reuse or recycle clothing, which reduces waste and the use of natural resources.

Emphatize

I conducted user research to find out the users’ knowledge about fast fashion and their shopping behaviour

User Research Findings

> 50%

of the participants are aware of the ongoing issue with the fast fashion industry, however, not many have decided to act upon it due to different factors, like prices and accessibility.

> 40%

of the participants are willing to swap their clothing with others in a safe manner.

> 50%

of participants already donate their unused clothing

Ideation & Low-fi Wireframe

During our brainstorming session, we explore different features and how it should be placed as we want to offer an easy to use and effective application.

Final Solution

Swapping

Users can choose their clothing preferences (measurements, gender, colour, material, etc) and swap with others who match their preferences, instead of throwing it away.

My Closet

Users can create outfits from the clothes save (either from their closet or from the homepage), mix and match and see how they can make an outfit!

Since they are finding new ways to wear their old clothing, it reduces waste as there is no longer the need to buy new clothes and throw out old ones.

Upcycling

The upcycling feature partners up with local services like tailors so that users are able to pay commissions to have their clothes upcycled.This allows reduce waste of unwanted clothes and reuse it by having tailors recycle and redesign their clothes into a better one.

Users are also able to take free classes on how to upcycle their clothes, so that they don’t need to depend on fast fashion industries to style themselves.

  • It is not easy to figure out how to incorporate a socially responsible lifestyle to an technological application that people can use everyday
