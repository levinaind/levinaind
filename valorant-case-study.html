Valorant Redesign

a block system concept focusing to decrease toxicity within the gaming community

Project Type

Personal Project

Role

UX Design, UX Research

Duration

3 weeks (Mar 2021)

Tools

Figma

Overview

Valorant is a 5v5 multiplayer character-based shooter developed by Riot Games. As of 2022, there are 15 millions monthly active users playing Valorant. With the rise of active players, there is also a rise of toxicity within the game. In order to reduce the toxic environment players face, I designed a concept to solve this issue.

Problem

How can we implement features to reduce toxicity in Valorant?

While Riot Games have implemented a ‘report’ and ‘mute’ feature, my challenge is to explore different ways they can implement to reduce toxicity in Valorant.

Solution

I designed a block system where when a user is blocked, it will ensure that the user will not be queued in the same team as the blocked user within a week time.

Emphatize

Despite being a Valorant gamer myself, I conducted user interviews and secondary research from the internet to gain more understanding about how players feel.

User research findings:

"Even after muting them (toxic teammates), they’ll know, troll around and throw my games."

"I just stop using mic (to speak in game) because my teammates would either harass me or throw the games just because I am a girl."

"I faced a mysoginistic teammate, reported him, and then the next game I was queued into the same teammate again."

Secondary Research

To validate my findings, I conducted some secondary research through Google, Twitter, and Reddit on the Valorant community regarding their experience with toxic players.

Findings:

  1. 411 300 players have been ‘mic cut’ due to being verbally or textually abusive
  2. A lot of players do not solo-queue (even a Riot Developer) because they are scared of being harassed

Here are the major pain points:

  1. Report system takes time to process - it is not an instant feedback
  2. Players can be queued with the toxic teammates twice in a row, even after reporting
  3. Solo-queue players feel like playing a game of jackpot everytime they queue, as they don’t know their team will be toxic or not
  4. Female players feel scared to use mic, as they are scared to be harassed or get team sabotaged

Competitive Analysis

I did in-depth research on other first-person shooters game in the market, and find out how they tackle toxicity. Blizzard’s Overwatch, implements a block system, where once a player is blocked, it removes their text and voice chat from being seen and heard, but it does not prevent being teamed up with the player. Another feature they implement is the endorsement system, where at the end of the game, users can endorse other users that makes positive contributions to the match. High-endorsed players will get queued with other high-endorsed players which creates an incentive to be a positive teammate.

Problem: Players should not get penalized for “dodging” toxic teammates

With the Flag teammates system, when players get queued with flagged teammates, it is ultimately up to the player to dodge. However, in Valorant, when players dodge a lot of the time, they will get penalized. This is not an effective solution as players should not get penalized for leaving themselves out of toxic situations.

Iteration #2

Sketch

I decided to brainstorm more to come up with other possible solution.

Mock-up

I designed a block feature, where players can block other players, which will make players not be able to queue with the blocked players.

New In-Game Match Settings Screen:



Problem: In high elo (higher ranked players), players may use the block list to avoid playing individuals they would not have a good time going against.

When they abuse this system, it will cause a long queue time for players.

Final Solution

Block System

The block feature ensures blocked players not to be queued in your team. They can still be queued against you.

Block Decay

Once a player blocks another player, there will be a 'block expiry' of one week. So within that one week, the players won't be queued to the same team. This week period provide enough time for a report feedback.

Block Up to 3 Players

A player can only block up to 3 players at a time.

What I Learned

  • User experience design is not always a linear process, since the users are apart of the design cycle, we need to use different approaches to solve a problem.
  • Always test my design with real users, because based on the data, I will notice the flaws of my design that I wouldn’t have before.
  • Designing the UI of a game is different than the typical mobile app UI, as there is no standardized template that I can refer to.
